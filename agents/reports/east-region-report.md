## 2. Team-by-Team Deep Dives

### (1) Duke Blue Devils — 31-3, KenPom #1

**Key Stats:**
- AdjO: 128.7 (top 5 nationally), AdjD: 89.9 (top 5 nationally)
- Only team ranked top-5 in BOTH offensive and defensive efficiency
- Turnover rate: ~9 per game (elite ball security)

**Key Players:**
- **Cooper Flagg** (Fr.): 18.9 PPG, 7.5 RPG, 4.1 APG, 1.5 SPG, 1.3 BPG — consensus All-American, projected #1 NBA pick. Sprained ankle in ACC Tournament (March 13) but cleared for NCAA opener.
- **Tyrese Proctor**: Elite perimeter scoring and defense
- **Kon Knueppel**: Reliable wing scorer
- **Khaman Maluach**: 7-footer providing rim protection

**Style:** Elite on both ends. Mistake-free ball-handling, elite perimeter defense combined with rim protection. Half-court execution with transition bursts.

**Coaching:** Jon Scheyer — second year as head coach, inheriting the Duke tournament pedigree. Team is well-organized and disciplined.

**Injury Risk:** Flagg's ankle is the only concern. Cleared to play, but re-injury risk elevates uncertainty slightly.

**Assessment:** Clear #1 in the region and national title contender. The gap between Duke and the field in this region is significant (~8 KenPom points over Alabama).

---

### (2) Alabama Crimson Tide — 25-8, KenPom #6

**Key Stats:**
- AdjO: Top 4 nationally (KenPom offensive efficiency)
- Leads Division I in PPG (91.4)
- Pace: 75.2 possessions/40 min (fastest in the country)
- AdjD: Weak relative to their overall ranking — defense is the Achilles heel

**Key Players:**
- **Mark Sears** (5th-year Sr.): 18.6 PPG, 5.0 APG — All-American guard. 3P% dipped from 43.6% to 32.9% this season, but volume remains high (7 3PA/game).
- **VJ Edgecombe-related transfer talent** on supporting cast

**Style:** Up-tempo, high-octane offense that pushes pace relentlessly. Perimeter-oriented with heavy 3-point volume. Defense prioritizes perimeter over paint protection, leaving interior exposed.

**Coaching:** Nate Oats — strong tournament track record, took Alabama to Final Four in 2024.

**Strengths:** When shots fall, this offense is nearly unstoppable. Elite transition play.

**Weaknesses:** Paint defense is exploitable. If 3s aren't falling, the offense can stall. Inconsistent defensive effort across a long season.

**Assessment:** Legitimate Elite 8 contender but vulnerable to disciplined defensive teams that slow pace and attack the paint.

---

### (3) Wisconsin Badgers — 26-9, KenPom #13

**Key Stats:**
- AdjO: #13 nationally (Final Four-caliber offense per KenPom)
- AdjD: #27 nationally (serviceable but not elite)
- Gave up 80+ points in 9 games (5-4 record)

**Key Players:**
- **John Tonje**: 19.6 PPG, 5.3 RPG — consensus 2nd-team All-American. 38.8% from 3, 90.9% FT. High-volume shot creator who gets to the free-throw line at an elite rate.

**Style:** Efficient half-court offense built around Tonje's shot creation. Wisconsin's traditional pace-control identity has loosened — this team can score but must survive shootouts when defense breaks down.

**Coaching:** Greg Gard — experienced tournament coach, understands Wisconsin's identity.

**Strengths:** Elite shot creation through Tonje. Good FT shooting in close games. Efficient half-court sets.

**Weaknesses:** Defense is porous against elite scoring teams. Can be outgunned in track meets. Limited depth concerns.

**Assessment:** Dangerous offensive team but defense puts a ceiling on their run. Sweet 16 is realistic; Elite 8 requires defensive improvement.

---

### (4) Arizona Wildcats — 22-12, KenPom #14

**Key Stats:**
- AdjO: #14, AdjD: #30
- 10 Quad 1 victories (impressive resume despite 12 losses)
- 4th-toughest schedule in the country

**Key Players:**
- **Caleb Love**: 16.6 PPG — March Madness veteran (Final Four with UNC). Career-best shooting (.413 FG%), 1.6 AST/TO ratio.
- **Henri Veesaar**: 7-footer providing interior presence

**Style:** Versatile offense with both perimeter and interior options. Size advantage in many matchups with Veesaar. Tournament-tested backcourt.

**Coaching:** Tommy Lloyd — innovative offensive mind, building Arizona's brand.

**Strengths:** Battle-tested with elite wins (BYU, Texas Tech, Kansas, Baylor). Size + backcourt experience. Love is a proven tournament performer.

**Weaknesses:** Inconsistent 3-point shooting (losses to Wisconsin, Duke, Houston came when shots didn't fall). 12 losses is a lot for a 4-seed; volatility is real.

**Assessment:** High ceiling, low floor. Could make a run on Caleb Love's back, or could lose early if shots aren't falling. Classic "boom or bust" 4-seed.

### (5) Oregon Ducks — 24-9, KenPom #31

**Key Stats:**
- AdjO: #40, AdjD: #30
- NET: #29
- 8 Quad 1 wins (8-7 in Q1 games)

**Key Players:**
- **Nate Bittle** (Sr.): 14.1 PPG, 7.4 RPG, 2.2 BPG — 7-footer, all-conference center
- **Jackson Shelstad**: Lead guard providing playmaking

**Style:** Dana Altman's unique defensive scheme creates disruption. Size advantage with Bittle and Kwame Evans (6-9). Good FT shooting. Physical inside play.

**Coaching:** Dana Altman — master of getting teams deep into March. Very experienced.

**Strengths:** Size (Bittle at 7-0, Evans at 6-9), defensive versatility, coaching.

**Weaknesses:** Offense can go cold; not elite scoring. KenPom #40 on offense is pedestrian for a 5-seed.

**Assessment:** Solid but unspectacular. Should handle Liberty but could struggle against higher seeds. Altman's coaching gives a ceiling-raising factor.

### (6) BYU Cougars — 24-9, KenPom #22

**Key Stats:**
- AdjO: #11 nationally (elite pick-and-roll offense)
- On a 9-game winning streak in last 10

**Key Players:**
- **Richie Saunders** (Jr.): 16.0 PPG, 4.4 RPG — 1st-team All-Big 12, 43.3% from 3
- **Egor Demin** (Fr.): 10.3 PPG, 5.4 APG — projected NBA pick, elite passer

**Style:** Elite pick-and-roll offense ranking 11th nationally in offensive efficiency. Kevin Young's first year as coach has been impressive. Balanced scoring with multiple threats.

**Coaching:** Kevin Young — first-year HC but inheriting a talented roster. College coaching newness is a risk factor in March.

**Strengths:** Offensive firepower and shooting (Saunders at 43.3% from 3). Multiple playmakers. Hot streak entering tournament.

**Weaknesses:** First-year coach in first NCAA Tournament. Defense not as strong as offense. Can they execute in pressure situations?

**Assessment:** Dangerous offensive team, but coaching inexperience in March is a real concern. The VCU matchup is a genuine toss-up.

### (7) Saint Mary's Gaels — 28-5, KenPom #19

**Key Stats:**
- AdjD: #8 nationally (elite defense)
- Scoring defense: 60.7 PPG allowed
- Offensive rebound %: 40.5% (2nd nationally)
- Opponents: 31.2% from 3, 45.8% from 2

**Key Players:**
- **Paulius Murauskas**: 8.0 RPG — anchors rebounding
- Balanced scoring approach; no single star but deep rotation

**Style:** Randy Bennett's hallmark: slow pace, elite defense, rebounding dominance, minimal turnovers (15.1% TO rate). They grind opponents down and win ugly.

**Coaching:** Randy Bennett — 24-year tenure, deep tournament experience. Master of game preparation.

**Strengths:** Elite defense and rebounding. Low turnover rate. Disciplined execution. Tournament-experienced coach.

**Weaknesses:** Lack of a go-to scorer. Pace-control style can be broken by elite offenses that force tempo. WCC schedule doesn't prepare for top competition.

**Assessment:** Classic mid-major sleeper. Defense and rebounding travel well in March. Could give Alabama real problems with pace control. Potential Sweet 16 team.

## 4. Second Round and Beyond — Path Analysis

### Round of 32

**Duke vs. Baylor:** Duke's elite defense should contain Edgecombe. Duke by 15+.
**Pick: Duke** (mutation rate: 0.05)

**Alabama vs. Saint Mary's:** This is the critical second-round matchup. Saint Mary's defense (#8 nationally) vs Alabama's #4 offense. Saint Mary's will slow pace and grind. Alabama's 3-point variance (Sears at 32.9%) makes this volatile. But Alabama's sheer talent and athleticism should prevail in a close, grind-it-out game.
**Pick: Alabama** (mutation rate: 0.25 — Saint Mary's is a REAL threat)

**Wisconsin vs. VCU:** If VCU upsets BYU, they face Wisconsin. VCU's defense vs Wisconsin's Tonje-led offense. Tonje's ability to get to the FT line is key against VCU's physical defense.
**Pick: Wisconsin** (mutation rate: 0.20)

**Arizona vs. Oregon:** Pac-12/Big 12 rivalry rematch. Arizona's size with Veesaar vs Oregon's Bittle — a fascinating big-man duel. Caleb Love in March is dangerous. Arizona's ceiling is higher.
**Pick: Arizona** (mutation rate: 0.35 — Oregon is live)

### Sweet 16

**Duke vs. Alabama:** The marquee regional semifinal. Duke's balanced excellence vs Alabama's offensive explosiveness. Duke's defense should slow Alabama's pace. Flagg vs Sears is appointment television. Duke's home-region advantage in Newark matters.
**Pick: Duke** (mutation rate: 0.25)

**Wisconsin vs. Arizona:** Tonje vs Love — two All-Americans. Both teams can be streaky. Arizona's size advantage with Veesaar could matter. But Wisconsin's offensive efficiency and Tonje's shot creation are elite.
**Pick: Arizona** (mutation rate: 0.45 — very close game, Arizona's tournament experience and size edge)

### Elite 8

**Duke vs. Arizona:** Duke's elite defense vs Arizona's Caleb Love. Love is capable of erupting for 30+ in any game, but Duke's talent depth and Flagg's two-way dominance project to prevail. Duke's AdjEM advantage (~12.5 points) is substantial even against a KenPom top-15 team.
**Pick: Duke** (mutation rate: 0.30)
